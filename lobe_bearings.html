 About Lobe Bearings

The standard hydrodynamic journal bearings are widely used because of their simplicity. At high speeds, however, these types of bearings are unstable and can damage the machinery. Non-circular bearings can be used in these cases. Lobe bearings are one such type of non-circular bearings that can significantly increase load carrying capacity and stabilize the shaft.

 About Three Lobe Bearings

These bearings have three lobes placed at 120° to each other, with three 20° grooves between them that have holes on the top that act as the inlet for the lubricant.

The shaft is offset by a small amount from the centre of the bearing to maintain an eccentricity. The lubricant flows between the shaft's outer surface and the bearing's inner surface and gets pushed out of the system from the sides of the bearing.

 CFD and FSI Analysis

A system coupled between two-phase (liquid and vapour phase) Computational Fluid Dynamics and two-way Fluid Structure Interaction was used to measure the static pressure of the lubricant on the bearing and the shaft, and the geometrical deformation of the fluid and the solid domain.

Three-lobe bearing model and mesh

A complete model including the bearing, shaft, and fluid domain was modelled on Fusion 360 and meshed on Ansys Workbench.

Ansys Workbench Modules

Static structural and fluent modules coupled together using the system coupling module in Ansys Workbench. The bearing and the shaft model was imported into the static structural module and the fluid domain was imported into the fluent module.

Eccentricity ratios and materials used

24 combinations were created using these eccentricity ratios and materials, and analysed and compared.

Eccentricity ratios 0.25, 0.4, 0.6, 0.75
Lubricants ISO VG32, Water
Bearing Materials Brass, Steel, Bronze
Shaft Materials Steel

 Results

The following data for all the 24 combinations were obtained.

  • Static pressure of lubricant
  • Bearing deformation
  • Bearing von-misses stress

The following trend was observed in the combinations that were compared.

  • Static pressure, deformation, and von-misses stress increases with an increase in eccentricity ratio
  • ISO VG32 causes higher pressure and stress than Water
  • Deformation and stresses induced in the bearing were least when structural steel was used, followed by bronze, and then brass

Data obtained for 0.75 eccentricity ratio

Similar data was obtained for 0.25, 0.4, and 0.6 eccentricity ratios.
