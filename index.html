 Unmanned Aerial Vehicles

As part of the Structures Subsystem of AeroMIT, a student research project team, I designed the structure, built, and flew several prototype UAVs. The manufacturing process employed various in-house technologies including laser-cutting, vacuum bagging and hand-layup, and materials such as carbon fibre, glass fibre, balsa and monokote. Following are some of the UAVs my team and I designed.

SAE Aero Design East 2019

Objective: Micro Class UAV style aircraft built to deploy from a small container under 2 minutes, and carry payloads approximately three times its empty weight.

Structural Design: Focused on high payload fraction, low empty weight, high strength, moderate endurance, and compact packing inside the container. Utilizes high load bearing carbon fibre reinforced composites in key areas such as landing skids, tail strakes, fuselage, empennage and internal spars, and lightweight balsa wood and monokote sheeting in the wing and empennage-to-fuselage connector.

Prototype testing: Tested several combinations of rib and spar spacing, electronic system compatibility, payload loading configuration, wing and fuselage section splits, and centre of mass.
