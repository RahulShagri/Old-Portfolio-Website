 Unmanned Aerial Vehicles

As part of the Structures Subsystem of AeroMIT, an interdisciplinary student research project team, I facilitated in designing the structure, building, and flying several UAV prototypes. The fabricating process employed various in-house technologies including laser-cutting, vacuum bagging and hand-layup, and advanced materials such as carbon fiber, glass fiber, balsa and monokote. Following are some of the UAVs my team and I designed.

SAE Aero Design 2019 Competition
(World Rank 5)

Objective: Micro Class UAV style aircraft built to deploy from a small container under 90 seconds, and carry payloads approximately three times its empty weight.

Structural Design: High modularity, high payload fraction, high strength-to-weight ratio, low drag, and compact packing inside the container. Utilized high load-bearing carbon fiber reinforced composites in key areas such as landing skids, tail strakes, fuselage, empennage and internal spars, and lightweight balsa wood and lightweight plastic shrink wrap monokote film sheeting in the wing. Electronic system includes a brushless DC motor, nano-tech lithium-ion battery, electronic speed controller, battery eliminator circuit, and two digital and two analogue high torque servo motors.

Prototype testing: Tested several combinations of rib and spar spacing, electronic system compatibility, payload loading configuration, wing and fuselage section splits, centre of mass, component placement in the container, materials, and connector mechanisms.

Software used: Autodesk Fusion360

Twin Tail Dual Motor UAV

Objective: To demonstrate and analyze the use of two motors and two vertical stabilizer arrangement.

Structural Design: Precise placement of two motors and two vertical stabilizers to eliminate slipping or skidding in flight. Electronic system includes a 4S lithium-ion battery, two high rpm brushless DC motors, five high torque analogue servo motors, and two electronic speed controller with built-in battery eliminator circuit. Utilizes balsa in the fuselage, wing skeleton and tail, plywood in high-stress areas, and depron for wing sheeting.

Canard UAV

Objective: To demonstrate and analyse the canard configuration.

Structural Design: Tapered wing design with endplates incorporated, and carbon fiber reinforced composite rod to support the canard configuration. Electronic system includes a 4S lithium-ion battery, high rpm brushless DC motor, electronic speed controller with built-in battery eliminator circuit, and three high torque analogue servo motors. Utilizes balsa in the fuselage, wing skeleton and canard, plywood in high-stress areas, and lightweight plastic shrink wrap monokote film for wing sheeting.

A-Tail UAV

Objective: To demonstrate and analyse A-Tail empennage configuration.

Structural Design: Standard wing design with two carbon fiber reinforced composite rods supporting the A-Tail empennage. Electronic system includes a 4S lithium-ion battery, electronic speed controller with built-in battery eliminator circuit, high rpm brushless DC motors, and four high torque analogue servo motors. Utilizes balsa in the fuselage, wing skeleton and tail, plywood in high-stress areas, and depron for wing sheeting.

 Patent

A scissors-needle system for intra-cavitary hydro-dissection and excision of tissues.

Specification: Surgical instrument having a scissors-needle combination with a pair of blades and a needle spaced away from the scissors configured to inject a fluid. Inner sharp edges of blades made of conducting material and the outer blunt edges made of non-conducting material. Allows for extension and retraction of needle and rotation of scissor blades about one axis.

Objective: To be used in endoscopy surgery to remove small visible tumours from the bladder for improved diagnosis. Designed to reduce costs, be used in combination with other available surgical equipment, and overcome drawbacks of known instruments in use.

Software used: Siemens NX and KeyShot.

Published in the Indian Patent Office
Application number: 201941051409
